# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who keep an XTEINK X3 attached to the back of an Android phone and want useful information without turning the phone over and entering the distracting main screen. The first user is a technically comfortable owner willing to install a companion firmware build and grant narrowly explained Android permissions.

The primary contexts are focused work, where the phone is face-down and notifications must be triaged, and travel, where a boarding pass must remain visible and scannable even when the phone or X3 is conserving power.

## Product Purpose

Turn the X3 into the quiet, persistent side of the phone while retaining CrossPoint's reader. The Android app owns integrations, intelligence, privacy choices, and layout generation. The X3 owns reliable display, local timing, physical input, bounded cached state, and power transitions.

Success means a person can start focus in seconds, trust urgent exceptions without receiving ordinary notification noise, and put a usable boarding pass on the X3 without navigating an app launcher on the device.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, direct, trustworthy. The interface should feel like a small instrument: calm when nothing needs attention and unequivocal when action is required.

## Anti-references

- A phone-screen mirror shrunk onto e-ink.
- A launcher containing many tiny companion apps.
- A card-filled dashboard where every feature competes for attention.
- Decorative consumer-AI styling, chat-first interaction, or model decisions presented as certainty.
- Airport UI that sacrifices barcode size or quiet zone for visual decoration.

## Design Principles

1. One surface, one job: every state has a single dominant piece of information or action.
2. Phone computes; X3 persists: complexity and integrations stay on Android while the X3 remains deterministic and recoverable.
3. Progressive disclosure: the normal path is short, while permissions, rules, and expert controls remain available when needed.
4. Reliability outranks cleverness: deterministic safety rules, explicit timestamps, visible degraded states, and scanner-safe tickets take precedence over AI confidence or visual novelty.
5. Power behavior is part of the interface: static pinned content and connected live content are visibly distinct choices with honest consequences.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The Android app targets WCAG 2.2 AA behavior, supports system font scaling, TalkBack labels, adequate touch targets, high contrast, and reduced motion. Information is never communicated by color alone.

The X3 UI uses large high-contrast text, logical button labels, orientation-aware controls, plain status language, and no motion-dependent meaning. Boarding-pass validation prioritizes barcode module size and quiet zones. Dynamic text is bounded and truncated predictably rather than shrinking into unreadability.
